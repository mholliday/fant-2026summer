1. What FANT is

FANT is the database application for the Forensic Osteology Research Station (FOREST). It stores a record for each donor, mirroring the two paper forms used in the lab: the Skeletal Inventory form and the Skeletal Analysis (John A. Williams Collection) form. Each record keeps a full version history, so every change is tracked.

2. Logging in and out

  1. Open the app and click Log In in the top navigation bar.
  2. Enter the username and password given to you by an administrator, then submit.
  3. Once signed in, your name appears at the top right. Click Log Out when you're finished.
Don't have an account? Accounts are created by an administrator — see your lab admin.

3. The Dashboard

After you log in, the Dashboard lists all active donor records, 10 per page. Each row shows the Donor ID, who created it, when it was last modified, who modified it, and how many saved versions exist.

  • Open a record — click anywhere on a row.
  • Change pages — use Previous / Next at the bottom.
  • View archived donors — click View Archive. Click Back to Dashboard to return.

4. Searching for a donor

Use the search box above the list to find donors by their text content — for example notes, ancestry, exemplars used, or other free-text fields. Type a term and click Search. The search is fuzzy, so close spellings still match. Click Clear to return to the full list. Search works on the archive too while the archive is showing.

5. Viewing a donor record

A donor page is organized top to bottom:

  • Metadata — created/modified by and when, and the number of versions.
  • Reference Forms — download the blank original Skeletal Analysis and Skeletal Inventory forms for comparison.
  • Images — photographs attached to the record (see below).
  • Two tabs — Skeletal Inventory and Skeletal Analysis. Click a tab to open it; click it again to collapse it. Inside, each section (Cranium, Dentition, Long Bones, Osteometry, etc.) expands and collapses when you click its header.
  • Version History — at the bottom, a list of every saved change to the record.

6. Creating and editing donors

Requires write access. If you have it, you'll see the relevant buttons.

Create a new donor

  1. On the Dashboard, click + New Donor.
  2. Fill in the form. It's split into the same sections you see when viewing a record.
  3. Save the record when you're done.

Edit an existing donor

  1. Open the donor, then click Edit at the top.
  2. Make your changes and save.
Every save creates a new entry in the record's Version History, so earlier states are never lost.

7. Working with images

The Images panel sits above the tabs on both the view and edit pages. Images appear on the donor's record for anyone who can view it. What you can do depends on your access:

ActionHowWho
UploadUse the file picker at the top of the panel. You can select several files at once. Each must be a PNG, JPEG, GIF, or WebP and no larger than 15 MB. Write access
Add / edit a captionClick Add caption (or Edit caption) on an image card, type a description, and Save.Write access
DeleteClick the red Delete button on the image card and confirm.Write access
DownloadClick Download on the image card to save the original file to your computer.Anyone who can view the record
Deleting an image is permanent and can't be undone — you'll be asked to confirm first.

8. Archiving a donor

Requires write access. Archiving removes a donor from the main Dashboard without deleting it. Open the donor and click Archive. The record then appears under View Archive. Only an administrator can restore an archived donor or delete it permanently.

9. Exporting a PDF

On any donor page, click Export PDF to download a printable copy of the record. This works whether or not you have write access.

10. What you can and can't do

Your account has an access level set by an administrator. It controls what you see:

If your account has…You can…
Read accessView donor records, search, browse the archive, and export PDFs.
Write accessEverything above, plus create, edit, archive donors, and manage images.
Admin accessEverything above, plus the Admin Panel (users, backups, restoring/deleting archived records).

If a button described in this guide doesn't appear for you, your account doesn't have that level of access. Contact an administrator if you need it changed.
